Modelling & Networks Group

Applying mathematical rigor to complex systems, infectious diseases, financial markets, and strategic optimization.

About the Group

The Modelling and Networks group focuses on the intersection of mathematics and real-world systems. Our research spans across mathematical biology, game theory, network science, and optimization. We develop models to understand disease transmission, social-ecological dynamics, and the hidden structures within financial and complex social networks.

Our work often integrates traditional analytical methods with data-driven techniques, including machine learning and random matrix theory, to solve contemporary problems in sustainability and finance.

Research Themes

  • Mathematical Modelling
  • Game Theory
  • Complex Networks
  • Optimization
  • Epidemiology
  • Financial Networks
